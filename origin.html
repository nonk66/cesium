Origins of Cesium

Cesium was discovered by Robert Bunsen and Gustav Kirchhoff in 1860.

It was discovered using flame spectroscopy, and was the first element to be discovered spectroscopically.

It was discovered in Heidelberg, Germany.

Its name comes from the Latin word caesius, meaning sky blue, named for the blue lines of its spectrum.

Cesium was almost discovered in 1846 by Carl Plattner when he was examining pollucite, but he ran out of material before he could figure it out.
